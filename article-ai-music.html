AI is Threatening Real Music - and Artists Deserve Protection

Wednesday, November 26, 2025 • Maya EL Kordy

In today's music industry, I believe that artists and listeners everywhere are threatened by the rise of artificial intelligence which increasingly blurs the line between genuine creativity and machine-made sound. This issue, especially visible on platforms like TikTok in 2025, shows why stronger rules are urgently needed to protect authentic music.

The viral success of "RUN" by HAVEN in 2025 highlights this confusion. When the song surges online, many listeners assumed it was AI-generated because of its vocals, forcing the artist to confirm publicly that the track was human made. This reaction proves how easily real musicians can lose recognition for their own work.

Beyond this example, the Recording Industry Association of America has reported the rapid rise in unauthorized AI voice cloning allowing almost anyone to mimic an artist's voice without permission, threatening both careers and audience trust.

To protect creativity, governments should regulate AI-generated music and streaming platforms should clearly label AI content.
