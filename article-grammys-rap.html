Rap Takes the Lead at the Grammy Nominations

Saturday, December 5, 2025 • Samuel Elgrably

Rap and hip-hop artists are at the forefront of the 2026 Grammy Award nominations, with several major categories dominated by nominees from the genre, according to the Recording Academy.

Leading the pack is rapper Kendrick Lamar, who received nine nominations — including Album of the Year for GNX, Best Rap Album and major general field categories such as Record of the Year and Song of the Year. Other hip-hop artists like Clipse, Doechii and Tyler, The Creator also earned multiple nods across genre and general categories, reflecting strong industry recognition for rap music this year.

Nominees for Best Rap Song and Best Rap Performance include tracks from Cardi B, GloRilla, and Kendrick Lamar, among others, showcasing a range of contemporary hip-hop achievements. The nominations list was officially released on November 7, with the awards ceremony scheduled for February 1, 2026, at the Crypto.com Arena in Los Angeles.

Industry analysts say the prominence of rap and hip-hop in this year's Grammys highlights the genre's continued influence on mainstream music, as artists push creative boundaries and achieve commercial success.
