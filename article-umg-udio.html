Universal Music Group Pivots from Lawsuit to Partnership with AI Music Firm

Saturday, November 29, 2025 • Iwan Saleh

Universal Music Group announced on 7 November 2025 that it has reached a strategic agreement with AI-music company Udio following an earlier copyright lawsuit, signalling a major shift in how major labels are engaging with artificial intelligence in music production.

Under the settlement, the two companies will collaborate to create new AI-driven music products trained exclusively on Universal's catalogue, the label said in a joint statement. The original lawsuit, filed last year by major labels on behalf of artists, alleged that Udio's text-to-audio software trained on copyrighted tracks without permission. Universal and Udio have not disclosed the financial terms of the deal or how artists will be compensated under the new model.

Industry analysts warn that while the agreement may mark progress in formalising AI-music workflows, many details remain unclear and artist groups are closely watching developments. The deal comes amid broader upheaval in the music sector, as streaming economics, creator rights, and AI-driven tools increasingly dominate major label strategies. A recent report found that the UK music industry contributed £8 billion to the economy in 2024.

Experts say the deal could shape how AI and the music industry work together in the future.
