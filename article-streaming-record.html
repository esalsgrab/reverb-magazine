Global Music Streaming Reaches New Record in 2025 as Fans Turn to More Emotional Songs

Wednesday, November 19, 2025 • Maya EL Kordy

Music fans around the world streamed more music than ever before in 2025, setting a new global record on major platforms like Spotify and Apple Music, as more listeners chose songs with deeper stories and emotional meaning.

Industry reports show that people are no longer listening just for a nice beat or catchy melody. Instead, many listeners want music that portrays real life problems and feelings.

British artists are a big part of this trend. UK rapper Dave, known for his honest and detailed storytelling, saw a strong rise in streams this year, especially with his new album "The Boy Who Played the Harp". Songs such as Black and Lesley continue to reach millions because they explore serious topics like identity, safety and personal struggle.

Streaming companies also say younger listeners are searching more often for songs with strong messages, something they can relate to. Experts believe this change reflects a wider cultural move towards music that feels true and personal, rather than music made only for entertainment.

With streaming numbers going up, music leaders say this shift towards meaningful, story-based songs may shape the future of the global music industry for years to come.
